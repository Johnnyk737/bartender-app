About this recipe

The Manhattan arrived on the cocktail scene around the same time as the Martini, a product of the great saloons of America's Gilded Age. As ever with the great classic cocktails, creation myths abound. The Manhattan is popularly credited to New York's Manhattan Club in 1874, at a dinner for Lady Randolph Churchill – but she was in England at the time, christening her son, Winston. Two more plausible candidates are “A man named Black who kept a place ten doors below Houston Street on Broadway” in the 1860s according to William F. Mulhall, a bartender who plied his trade at New York’s famed Hoffman House. Or there’s the story that it was a certain Colonel Joe Walker, who ran one of the most notorious saloons in New Orleans, the Crescent Hall Saloon. He is supposed to have created the drink on a yachting trip with friends in New York. The first Manhattans were almost certainly made with rye whisky. In the aftermath of Prohibition, bourbon became popular. Too much peat, however, can overpower the drink, which is why classic blended whiskies with a balance of flavours are ideal: Johnnie Walker Black Label, Bells, J&B and Bushmills make a fine Manhattan. Variations on the Manhattan are myriad. The Dry Manhattan is made with dry vermouth; the Perfect Manhattan is made with a mixture of sweet and dry vermouths. According to one 1899 source, though, the true Manhattan is always made with sweet vermouth.

How to make

  1. Place The Singleton of Dufftown Malt Whisky, sweet vermouth and angostura bitters into a mixing glass.

    Using a jigger, measure 50ml The Singleton of Dufftown Malt Whisky, 25ml sweet vermouth and a dash of angostura bitters into a mixing glass.

  2. Fill the glass with ice, stir the mixture until well combined and strain into a martini glass.

    Fill mixing glass with ice and stir with a bar spoon. Using a strainer, strain into a martini glass.

  3. Garnish with an orange twist and maraschino cherry.

    Using chopping board and sharp knife slice an orange twist and add on top of the drink, along with one cherry, to garnish.

Grab a bite

Get some grub in before or while you’re drinking – it slows alcohol absorption.
