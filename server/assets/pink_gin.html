Pink Gin

199 2.5
Medium

Based on a backbone of juniper-edged, refreshing Gordon’s® London Dry Gin with subtle bitterness and a subtle citrus tang: the Pink Gin is one of the all-time classic cocktails, and yet it’s remarkably easy to make.

About this recipe

The Pink Gin has to be one of the easiest of all the classic cocktails for you to mix up, with its pink colour simply coming from a few drops of bitters, usually Angostura, mixed into some delicious gin. Classically the Pink Gin is stirred with ice in a mixing glass – or tumbler – but you can just as well serve this fine cocktail in a rocks glass with cubed ice. A wedge of lime will do just as well as lemon, while some bartenders add chilled water (approx. 30ml for this recipe). Just remember to go easy on the Angostura – bartenders are currently using plenty of bitters in their cocktails, but in the case of the Pink Gin we’re talking elegance and subtlety, so preserve the aromatics and flavours of the gin botanicals with just a few drops. The lemon peel twist then adds a gentle zestiness. Popular in the Royal Navy – gin and rum were served as a ration until the 1970s – the Pink Gin was originally used against stomach upsets, as Angostura Bitters were considered to have medicinal benefits. It then became fashionable in bars in England by the 1870s. Authors and filmmakers love the Pink Gin. Lottie Cassell, a character in 2010 Channel 4 TV series Any Human Heart offers the cocktail to Logan Mountstuart; author John le Carré makes Pink Gin the drink of choice for Jerry Westerby in Tinker, Tailor, Soldier, Spy. And James Bond orders a Pink Gin in Ian Fleming’s novel The Man with the Golden Gun. Nowadays this cocktail is ideal for those occasions when you want a classic serve in a tumbler or glass. But the Pink Gin can also be scaled up very easily just by serving in a Martini glass or coupe –present at your cocktail party and really impress your guests!

How to make

  1. Add ice to a glass.

    Place ice cubes into a mixing glass or tumbler until ½ full.

  2. Add Gordon’s London Dry Gin and Angostura Bitters.

    Using a jigger, measure 50ml Gordon’s London Dry Gin into the glass. Add 2 dashes Angostura Bitters.

  3. Stir with a spoon.

    Stir the mixture thoroughly using a bar spoon until well combined.

  4. Strain into a glass.

    Using a cocktail strainer, strain into a Martini glass or coupe.

  5. Garnish with lemon.

    Using a sharp knife and chopping board, cut a twist of lemon and place on top of the drink to garnish.

Grab a bite

Get some grub in before or while you’re drinking – it slows alcohol absorption.
