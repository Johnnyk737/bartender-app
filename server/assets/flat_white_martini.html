Flat White Martini

348 3
Medium

From coffee chains across the land comes the craze for the flat white, and we’ve captured it in cocktail form with the Flat White Martini. So create your very own coffee serve in the kitchen – just don’t charge your guests for a glass!

About this recipe

A souped-up version of the Espresso Martini, which itself was born out of the Irish Coffee, the Flat White Martini recipe is the culmination of so much coffee love. Bring out the barista in you along with that bartender! Subtly sweet with a hint of bitterness, it’s in the sweet spot where cocktails and coffee meet. So pour delicious Baileys alongside a shot of espresso or black coffee, add vodka and blend these very different ingredients the best way – with a good shake. Meanwhile if you can find some spare coffee beans in your cupboard, use a single one for a little bit of garnish/showing off! Dancing with the Flat White Martini in its elegant, tall-stemmed glass tends to be a delicate balancing act. So serve for early arrivals at your house party – reward them in style. How about a regular Martini and a French Martini too? Now that’s some Martini Madness. But you can really go flat out at your dinner party. Instead of serving coffee with chocolate, offer a Flat White Martini at the end of the meal. The Flat White Martini also makes a great alternative to Christmas pudding – and it’s nearly as filling! With the Flat White Martini you won’t leave guests feeling flat – they just know everything is going to be all white!

How to make

  1. Fill a shaker with ice.

    Add cubes of ice to a cocktail shaker.

  2. Pour Baileys Original Irish Cream, espresso and Smirnoff No.21 into the shaker.

    Using a jigger, measure 50ml Baileys Original Irish Cream and 25ml espresso and 25ml Smirnoff No.21 into the cocktail shaker.

  3. Shake until cold.

    Shake the mixture vigorously until the surface of the cocktail shaker feels chilled.

  4. Strain into a glass.

    Use a cocktail strainer to strain the mixture into a Martini glass.

  5. Top with coffee beans.

    Add three coffee beans on top of the drink to garnish.

Grab a bite

Get some grub in before or while you’re drinking – it slows alcohol absorption.
