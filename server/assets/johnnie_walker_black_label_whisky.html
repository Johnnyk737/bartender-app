Story

With origins in the 1870s Old Special Whisky from the Johnnie Walker family, Johnnie Walker Black Label was first crafted in the early 1900s as a masterful blend of the most premium whiskies. In 1909, it became simply ‘Black Label'. Flavour and heritage make Johnnie Walker Black Label a classic of the whisky world, the benchmark by which all other whiskies are measured.

Origin

Each bottle of Johnnie Walker Black Label draws whisky from the finest distilleries across Scotland, aged in whisky casks for at least 12 years. The whiskies are matured in a slightly different way, due to the intricacies of wood, climate and location. The master blender's skill lies in blending these together to create a whisky with an extraordinary range of flavour.

How to enjoy

Johnnie Walker Black Label Whisky is the perfectly balanced whisky, made for savouring over ice to soften some of the smokier notes, while keeping an intense depth of flavour. Or serve 50ml neat and have a carafe of water to adjust to taste. Try it with some dried fruits and nuts and if you want more, discover great Johnnie Walker whiskies here.
