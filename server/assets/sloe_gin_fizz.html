About this recipe

If a cocktail is fizzing away, that can only mean one thing: someone wants to celebrate. That’s the good news; even better is the Sloe Gin Fizz cocktail, the champagne cocktail without the champagne – and price tag. Splash Gordon’s Sloe Gin and a few other ingredients together instead. You can get fizzicle with your shaker and mix delicious sloe gin into lemon and sugar. No, a Fizz doesn’t mean a fizzy drink – a Fizz is actually the oldest of all cocktail recipes. All the way back in 1880 they were mixing their fizzes, employing hundreds of staff at bars in New Orleans to deal with demand. Your guests will be clamouring for more but you don’t need to stress – just shake up lots ahead of time for that birthday celebration or anniversary. Like all good fizzes and sours, the Fizz has the balancing act of sweet and sour – you can add the sparkling water at the end. Don’t forget that rich red colour, which marks out the Sloe Gin Fizz cocktail from other fizzes, made for romance or a date night. The Sloe Gin Fizz is nature’s cocktail recipe, originally made in autumn hedgerows where the sloe berries are grown. Enjoy those mouth-puckering flavours that can also be unveiled on a summer picnic. Don’t wait around for a celebration to come to you – create one with the Sloe Gin Fizz. But get a move on; too sloe and you’ll miss the opportunity.

How to make

  1. Add ice to a shaker.

    Fill a cocktail shaker with cubes of ice.

  2. Pour Gordon's Sloe Gin, lemon juice and sugar syrup into the shaker.

    Using a jigger, measure 50ml Gordon's Sloe Gin, 25ml lemon juice and 10ml sugar syrup into the shaker.

  3. Shake until cold.

    Shake the mixture vigorously until the surface of the cocktail shaker feels chilled.

  4. Strain into a tall glass.

    Use a cocktail strainer to strain the liquid into a tall glass or champagne flute filled with ice cubes.

  5. Pour in sparkling water.

    Add 100ml sparkling water or enough to fill to the top of the glass.

  6. Garnish with an orange slice and a cherry.

    With a sharp knife and a chopping board, cut a slice of orange and place in the drink along with one cherry to garnish.

Avoid top-ups

It’s easier to keep track of your drinks if you know how many full glasses you’re drinking.

Gordon's® Sloe Gin

Gordon’s Sloe Gin was first launched in 1908, but a fabulous new version has brought it back to life. A classic blend, this takes the dryness of Gordon’s with cassis sweetness in a clear bottle that shows off the rich, dark berry colour and great taste.
